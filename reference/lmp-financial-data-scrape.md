## Loan Programs

### Fixed-Rate Mortgage
**URL:** /fixed-rate-mortgage

> A reliable home loan where the interest rate remains constant throughout the entire loan term.

#### Types
**1) 30-Year Fixed-Rate Mortgage (30-Year FRM)**
- Ideal for lower monthly payments / extended terms.
- Competitive, stable rate.
- Down payment: as low as **3% to 5%**.
- Fast close: typically **14 to 21 days** from contract to keys.
- Eligibility: 3% down (low-balance) / 5% down (high-balance), credit score min **620**, within county conforming loan limits.

**2) 15-Year Fixed-Rate Mortgage (15-Year FRM)**
- Pay off faster, lower total interest.
- Lower interest rate than 30-Year FRM.
- Higher monthly payments (shorter term).

#### Benefits
1. Rate stability (predictable payments)
2. Long-term budgeting
3. Protection against interest-rate fluctuations

#### Eligibility
- Stable income
- Good credit score (min 620)
- Reasonable DTI
- Down payment varies by loan type

#### 30-yr vs 15-yr
> While a 15-year fixed-rate mortgage accrues less overall interest, the 30-year option offers lower monthly payments, providing more liquidity and flexibility.

#### Fixed vs ARM
> Unlike ARMs, 30-year fixed-rate mortgages offer stability with a constant interest rate. ARMs have fluctuating rates after an initial fixed period, introducing more uncertainty and risk.

### FHA Loans (Federal Housing Administration)
**URL:** /fha-loans

> Loans designed to assist homebuyers with more lenient credit and down payment requirements.

#### Pros
1. Lenient credit guidelines (credit score as low as **580**)
2. Lower interest rates (especially for lower-credit borrowers)
3. Down payment as low as **3.5%**

#### Cons
1. Mortgage insurance complexity (upfront + monthly MI)
2. Lower loan limits (county-specific)
3. Primary residences only

#### Down Payment
3.5% minimum; entire amount can come from gift funds; compatible with down-payment assistance & grants.

#### Quick-Hit Benefits
- Low interest rates
- 3.5% down payment
- Fast closing — 14 to 21 days
- Gift funds for costs

#### Eligibility
- Min credit score: **580**
- Min down payment: **3.5%**
- Max DTI: **56%**
- Primary residences only

**URL:** /va-loans

> Empowering American heroes with accessible, zero-down homeownership.

> Elevate your homeownership aspirations with financing tailored for high-value properties beyond conventional limits.

#### Benefits
1. Lowest jumbo loan rates available
2. Maximum loan amounts up to **$3 million**
3. Down payment as low as **10%**
4. **No PMI**

#### Eligibility
1. Down payment **10–20%**
2. Credit score **680+**
3. Minimum **6 months' reserves**
4. Stricter underwriting

Fixed-rate and ARM jumbo. Combination financing also offered as alternative.

### Adjustable-Rate Mortgage (ARM)
**URL:** /adjustable-rate-mortgage-arm

> Financial flexibility and optimal rates with an Adjustable-Rate Mortgage – Your key to a dynamic homeownership journey

#### Benefits
1. Best initial interest rates
2. Down payment as low as **5%**
3. Flexible payments (can decrease if market rates fall)
4. Tailored for short-term homeownership (5–10 years)

#### Eligibility
- 5% minimum down payment
- Credit score **620+**
- Adaptable credit requirements
- Lower monthly commitments during fixed period

#### How ARM rates are calculated
> ARM rates are calculated based on the index (commonly **SOFR**) and the lender-set margin, combining to form the fully indexed rate. Caps ensure protection during the adjustable period.

#### Pros
- Competitive initial rates
- Flexibility for refinancing
- Potential for reduced rates if market drops

#### Cons
- Market volatility risk
- Complex financial planning
- Considerable risk if rates rise

#### Best fit
ARMs: short-term homeowners, planned refinancers.
Fixed-rate: risk-averse, long-term homeowners, simplicity-seekers.

### USDA Mortgages
**URL:** /usda-mortgages

> Cultivating Homeownership Dreams in Rural and Suburban America.

#### Key Features
1. **No down payment** (100% financing)
2. Low interest rates
3. Flexible credit requirements
4. Geographic eligibility (rural + select suburban)

#### Eligibility
- Property must be in eligible rural/suburban USDA area
- Household income limits (location & family-size based)
- Must be U.S. citizen, qualified alien, or non-citizen national

#### Loan Types
1. **Section 502 Direct Loan** — direct USDA financing for low / very-low income
2. **Guaranteed Loan Program** — through approved lenders with USDA guarantee

#### Application Path
1. Find an approved USDA lender
2. Verify property + financial eligibility
3. Submit documents
4. Property inspection (USDA typically requires)
5. Final approval

### Reverse Mortgages
**URL:** /reverse-mortgages

> Loan that allows homeowners, usually seniors, to convert home equity into cash for living expenses

#### Eligibility
- Typically **age 62+**
- Own home outright or have substantial equity
- Must be primary residence

#### Benefits
1. **Financial flexibility** — lump sum, fixed monthly, or line of credit
2. **No monthly payments** to lender
3. **Stay in your home** while meeting tax/maintenance obligations

#### Pros
- Supplement retirement income
- Retain homeownership without monthly mortgage burden

#### Cons
- Accrued interest reduces equity
- Loan must be repaid on death/sale (impacts inheritance)

### Interest Only Mortgages
**URL:** /interest-only-mortgages

> Interest-only mortgages allow borrowers to pay only the interest for a set period, reducing initial monthly payments.

Initial interest-only period typically **5 to 10 years**, then both principal and interest.

#### Eligibility
Generally borrowers with good credit; suits irregular income or those expecting future earning growth.

#### Benefits
1. Lower initial monthly payments
2. Flexibility for those expecting future income growth or planning to sell

#### Pros
- Initial affordability
- Investment opportunity (redirect savings)

#### Cons
- Higher payments after IO period ends
- Potential for negative amortization if home values fall
- Limited equity buildup during IO period
